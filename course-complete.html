Module 1: Project Overview & Objectives

Learning Objectives: By the end of this module, you will understand the WHO SkinNTD AI project, the governance structure, your role as a contributing clinician, and the ethical principles guiding this initiative.

1.1 Why This Project Matters

The Challenge: Leprosy remains underdiagnosed in many regions worldwide. Despite being curable, delayed diagnosis leads to permanent disabilities, social stigma, continued transmission, and economic hardship for affected families.

The Role of AI: Artificial Intelligence can help bridge the diagnostic gap by assisting primary health workers who lack specialist training, providing consistent diagnostic support across regions, enabling earlier detection and treatment, and reducing the burden on scarce dermatology specialists.

The Need for Quality Data: AI systems learn from examples. To create an effective diagnostic tool, we need thousands of diverse clinical images from different skin tones (Fitzpatrick types I-VI), various geographic regions, different disease stages, and properly anonymized and consented images.

Your Contribution: As a contributing clinician, you play a critical role in this global health initiative. Each high-quality image you submit helps train AI systems that will benefit patients in underserved communities, support healthcare workers with limited resources, and contribute to the global effort to eliminate leprosy.

1.2 Understanding the AI Training Process

How AI Learns from Images: Machine learning systems analyze visual patterns in thousands of clinical images, learn correlations between visual features and diagnoses, build predictive models, and improve accuracy through exposure to diverse examples.

What Makes a Good Training Image:

  • Clinically informative: Shows the condition clearly
  • Properly labeled: Includes accurate metadata and diagnosis
  • Anonymized: Contains no identifying patient information
  • Diverse: Represents different populations and presentations
  • High quality: In focus, well-lit, properly framed

1.3 Governance Structure

The WHO SkinNTD AI Image Collection Initiative operates through a four-level governance structure:

Level Parties Responsibilities
Level 1 WHO - TLM International Strategic oversight, ethical standards, licensing, global health alignment
Level 2 TLM - Partner Organizations Partnership agreements, training, compliance, data security
Level 3 Partner Org - Clinicians Authorization, institutional support, quality control
Level 4 Clinician - Patient Direct patient care, informed consent, dignity, clinical judgment
Your Position: You are at Level 4 with direct responsibility to patients. You have the authority to photograph eligible patients but must follow institutional protocols and maintain your ethical obligations to patients. Patient welfare always comes first.

1.4 Project Objectives

Primary Objective: To build a diverse, high-quality dataset of clinical images of leprosy and skin NTDs for training AI diagnostic systems for deployment in resource-limited healthcare settings.

Specific Goals:

  • Collect 10,000+ images across diverse populations
  • Ensure geographic diversity from all WHO regions
  • Represent all skin phototypes (Fitzpatrick I-VI)
  • Include multiple disease presentations and stages
  • Maintain highest ethical standards throughout

1.6 Ethical Principles

The project is guided by four core ethical principles:

  1. Respect for Persons: Autonomy, dignity, and extra protection for vulnerable individuals
  2. Beneficence: Benefit patients through improved diagnosis and contribute to global health
  3. Non-maleficence: Do no harm, protect from stigma, prevent privacy breaches, avoid exploitation
  4. Justice: Fair distribution of benefits, inclusion of diverse populations, global health equity

1.7 Knowledge Check

Question 1: What is the PRIMARY goal of the WHO SkinNTD AI Image Collection Initiative?

Correct! The initiative aims to build AI diagnostic tools to help healthcare workers in resource-limited settings. This is a non-commercial public health initiative designed to support, not replace, healthcare professionals.

Question 2: In the governance structure, at which level do you (the clinician) have direct responsibility to patients?

Correct! While you're employed/associated with Level 3 (Partner Organization), your direct responsibility is Level 4 - the Clinician-Patient relationship where you have ethical obligations to patient welfare.

Question 3: Which of the following is NOT a responsibility of contributing clinicians?

Correct! Images must NEVER be shared outside the secure system. This would violate patient privacy and the terms of participation. All images must remain strictly confidential.
