## Questions by Module

### Module 1: Project Overview (Questions 1-4)

**Question 1 (Multiple Choice)**
What is the PRIMARY goal of the WHO SkinNTD AI Image Collection Initiative?

A) To create a commercial database of skin disease images
B) To train AI systems for improved leprosy and skin NTD diagnosis
C) To replace dermatologists with AI technology
D) To develop new medications for leprosy

**Correct Answer: B**
**Explanation:** The initiative aims to build AI diagnostic tools to help healthcare workers in resource-limited settings. This is a non-commercial public health initiative designed to support, not replace, healthcare professionals.

---

**Question 2 (Multiple Choice)**
In the governance structure, at which level do you (the clinician) have direct responsibility to patients?

A) Level 1: WHO oversight
B) Level 2: Partnership management
C) Level 3: Partner Organization - Clinician
D) Level 4: Direct patient relationship

**Correct Answer: D**
**Explanation:** While you're employed/associated with Level 3 (Partner Organization), your direct responsibility is Level 4 - the Clinician-Patient relationship where you have ethical obligations to patient welfare.

---

**Question 3 (True/False)**
As a contributing clinician, you can withdraw from the project if you have ethical concerns.

**Correct Answer: True**
**Explanation:** You maintain professional autonomy. If you have concerns about ethical aspects of the project, you can discuss them with your supervisor or withdraw while maintaining your duty to existing patients.

---

**Question 4 (Multiple Choice)**
Which of the following is NOT a core ethical principle guiding this project?

A) Respect for Persons
B) Beneficence
C) Profit Maximization
D) Justice

**Correct Answer: C**
**Explanation:** This is a non-profit public health initiative. The core ethical principles are Respect for Persons, Beneficence, Non-maleficence (do no harm), and Justice. Profit is not a consideration.

---

### Module 2: Clinical Photography (Questions 5-8)

**Question 5 (Multiple Choice)**
Which metadata field is used to ensure skin tone diversity in the AI training dataset?

A) Age Group
B) Clinical Form (Ridley-Jopling Classification)
C) Phototype (Fitzpatrick Scale)
D) Operational Classification

**Correct Answer: C**
**Explanation:** The Fitzpatrick Scale (I-VI) documents skin tone to ensure AI works across diverse populations. This is critical for equitable AI performance across all skin types.

---

**Question 6 (True/False)**
You should keep copies of patient photos on your phone for reference after uploading to the secure system.

**Correct Answer: False**
**Explanation:** Delete all photos from personal devices after secure upload. This is a security requirement. Keeping copies increases breach risk and violates data minimization principles.

---

**Question 7 (Multiple Choice)**
When photographing lesions on private body areas, which is required?

A) A full-body photograph for context
B) Explicit consent specifically for that body region
C) Photography with multiple staff members present
D) Standard consent form is sufficient

**Correct Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Private areas require explicit, specific consent. General consent is insufficient. Full-body photos should be avoided, privacy should be maximized, and staff presence minimized.

---

**Question 8 (Multiple Choice)**
What is the minimum recommended image resolution for submissions?

A) 640 x 480 pixels
B) 1280 x 720 pixels
C) 1920 x 1080 pixels
D) 3840 x 2160 pixels

**Correct Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Full HD (1920 x 1080) is the minimum required resolution. Higher resolution is preferred if available, as it provides better detail for AI training.

---

### Module 3: Informed Consent (Questions 9-12)

**Question 9 (Scenario-Based)**
You need to photograph a patient's leg lesions. The patient speaks a language you don't understand. Their daughter offers to translate. What should you do?

A) Accept the daughter's offer - family knows best
B) Wait for a professional interpreter or use phone service
C) Proceed with simple gestures - photography is universal
D) Ask the daughter to convince her parent to participate

**Correct Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Professional interpretation is required for informed consent. Family members may not translate accurately, may add opinions, or the patient may not feel free to refuse. Phone interpretation services are an acceptable alternative.

---

**Question 10 (True/False)**
Consent is a one-time event that happens before photography begins.

**Correct Answer: False**
**Explanation:** Consent is an ongoing process that continues throughout. You should check in regularly and respect withdrawal at any time. Non-verbal cues matter.

---

**Question 11 (Scenario-Based)**
You have consent and have photographed one lesion. As you prepare to photograph a second lesion, the patient says "Actually, I changed my mind. I don't want to do this anymore." What should you do?

A) Explain that you're almost done and encourage them to continue
B) Ask why they changed their mind
C) Stop immediately and thank them
D) Suggest taking just one more photo

**Correct Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Consent can be withdrawn at ANY time, even mid-procedure. You must stop immediately, respect their decision completely, and thank them for what they've provided. Never pressure or question their decision.

---

**Question 12 (Multiple Choice)**
Which is a red flag that consent may not be voluntary?

A) Patient asks detailed questions about the project
B) Patient looks to family member before answering
C) Patient takes time to read the consent form
D) Patient requests clarification on data usage

**Correct Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Looking to others for approval may indicate pressure or lack of autonomy. The other options show engagement and responsible decision-making.

---

### Module 4: Anonymization & Privacy (Questions 13-16)

**Question 13 (Multiple Choice)**
Which of the following does NOT need to be removed from clinical photos before submission?


A) Patient's hospital wristband
B) Clinical lesion being documented
C) Distinctive tattoo near lesion
D) Background showing recognizable landmark

**Correct Answer: B**
**Explanation:** The clinical lesion is the subject and must be visible for AI training. All identifying features (wristbands, tattoos, backgrounds) must be removed or obscured.

---

**Question 14 (True/False)**
If you discover a privacy breach, you should try to fix it yourself before reporting.

**Correct Answer: False**
**Explanation:** Report breaches immediately to your supervisor. Don't try to handle alone. Early reporting allows proper containment and response. Delayed reporting makes breaches worse.

---

**Question 15 (Multiple Choice)**
What is the first step in the anonymization process?

A) Apply digital blurring
B) Review immediately after photography
C) Upload to secure system
D) Remove metadata

**Correct Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Immediate review allows you to delete photos with obvious identifiers and retake if needed, before spending time editing. Prevention is better than correction.

---

**Question 16 (Multiple Choice)**
Which anonymization technique is generally most secure?

A) Slight blurring
B) Heavy blurring
C) Cropping out the identifier
D) Pixelation

**Correct Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Cropping removes the identifier completely. Some blurs can potentially be reversed. Cropping is irreversible and most secure when clinically appropriate.

---

### Module 5: Safeguarding (Questions 17-20)

**Question 17 (Scenario-Based)**
A parent has consented for their 10-year-old child's photos, but the child says "I don't want to" and looks uncomfortable. What should you do?

A) Proceed because parent has legal authority
B) Stop - respect the child's wishes
C) Ask the parent to convince the child
D) Take the photos quickly before the child objects more

**Correct Answer: B**
**Explanation:** While parents provide legal consent, children should provide assent. If they refuse or are uncomfortable, respect their bodily autonomy. Never force a child to be photographed against their will.

---

**Question 18 (True/False)**
You should report suspected child abuse only if you are 100% certain.

**Correct Answer: False**
**Explanation:** Mandatory reporting requires you to report suspicions, not just confirmed cases. Let authorities investigate. Your role is to report observations, not to investigate or confirm abuse.

---

**Question 19 (Multiple Choice)**
A patient becomes distressed during photography of a private body area. What should you do FIRST?

A) Complete the photography quickly
B) Ask what's wrong while continuing
C) Stop immediately and cover the patient
D) Call for more staff to help

**Correct Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Stop immediately and cover the patient. Then address their concerns. Never continue if patient is distressed. Dignity and safety come first.

---

**Question 20 (Scenario-Based)**
While reviewing photos, you notice one shows a child with multiple bruises in various stages of healing not related to the skin condition. What should you do?

A) Submit it to the project - doctors should see this
B) Ignore it - it's not related to leprosy
C) Follow institutional child protection protocols immediately
D) Ask the parent about the bruises first

**Correct Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Do NOT submit to project. Follow child protection reporting protocols immediately. This takes precedence over the image collection. Document and report to appropriate authorities.
