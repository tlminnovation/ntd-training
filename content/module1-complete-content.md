## Section 1: Welcome and Introduction

### Opening Statement
Welcome to the SkinApp Training Course for the WHO SkinNTD AI Image Collection Initiative. This course will prepare you to contribute clinical images of leprosy and skin Neglected Tropical Diseases (NTDs) that will be used to train artificial intelligence systems for improved diagnosis in resource-limited settings.

### Why This Project Matters

**The Challenge:**
Leprosy remains underdiagnosed in many regions worldwide. Despite being curable, delayed diagnosis leads to:
- Permanent disabilities
- Social stigma and discrimination
- Continued transmission in communities
- Economic hardship for affected families

**The Role of AI:**
Artificial Intelligence can help bridge the diagnostic gap by:
- Assisting primary health workers who lack specialist training
- Providing consistent diagnostic support across different regions
- Enabling earlier detection and treatment
- Reducing the burden on scarce dermatology specialists

**The Need for Quality Data:**
AI systems learn from examples. To create an effective diagnostic tool, we need:
- Thousands of diverse clinical images
- Images from different skin tones (Fitzpatrick types I-VI)
- Images from various geographic regions
- Images showing different disease stages and presentations
- Properly anonymized and consented images

### Your Contribution Matters
As a contributing clinician, you play a critical role in this global health initiative. Each high-quality image you submit helps train AI systems that will:
- Benefit patients in underserved communities
- Support healthcare workers with limited resources
- Contribute to the global effort to eliminate leprosy
- Improve outcomes for skin NTD patients worldwide

## Section 2: Understanding the AI Training Process

### How AI Learns from Images
Machine learning systems for medical diagnosis work by:
1. **Analyzing visual patterns** in thousands of clinical images
2. **Learning correlations** between visual features and diagnoses
3. **Building predictive models** that can classify new images
4. **Improving accuracy** through exposure to diverse examples

### What Makes a Good Training Image
For AI training, images must be:
- **Clinically informative**: Show the condition clearly
- **Properly labeled**: Include accurate metadata and diagnosis
- **Anonymized**: Contain no identifying patient information
- **Diverse**: Represent different populations and presentations
- **High quality**: In focus, well-lit, properly framed

### The Data Pipeline
Your submitted images go through:
1. **Secure upload** to encrypted servers
2. **Quality review** by clinical specialists
3. **Anonymization** verification
4. **Metadata validation**
5. **Inclusion in training dataset**
6. **AI model training**
7. **Validation and testing**
8. **Deployment** in clinical tools

## Section 3: Governance Structure

### The Responsibility Chain
The WHO SkinNTD AI Image Collection Initiative operates through a four-level governance structure:

**Level 1: WHO - TLM International**
- WHO provides strategic oversight and mandate
- Establishes ethical and technical standards
- Licenses the use of collected data for AI training
- Ensures alignment with global health goals

**Level 2: TLM International - Partner Organizations**
- Formal partnership agreements established
- Defines roles, responsibilities, and data sharing
- Provides training and technical support
- Ensures compliance with ethical standards
- Manages data access and security

**Level 3: Partner Organization - Clinicians**
- Organization authorizes clinicians to participate
- Provides institutional support and oversight
- Ensures local regulatory compliance
- Manages quality control at site level
- You are at this level, with direct patient responsibility

**Level 4: Clinician - Patient**
- Direct clinical relationship
- Ethical obligation to patient welfare
- Responsibility for informed consent
- Duty to maintain dignity and confidentiality
- Authority to make clinical judgments

### Your Position and Authority
As a clinician participating in this project:
- You have the authority to photograph eligible patients
- You must follow institutional protocols
- You report to your partner organization
- You maintain your ethical obligations to patients
- You can withdraw from the project if you have concerns

### Accountability Framework
**Your accountability includes:**
1. **Clinical accountability** to your institution and patients
2. **Ethical accountability** to professional standards
3. **Project accountability** to partner organization and TLM
4. **Legal accountability** to local data protection regulations

## Section 4: Project Objectives and Scope

### Primary Objective
To build a diverse, high-quality dataset of clinical images of leprosy and skin NTDs that can be used to train AI diagnostic systems for deployment in resource-limited healthcare settings.

### Specific Goals
1. **Collect 10,000+ images** across diverse populations
2. **Ensure geographic diversity** from all WHO regions
3. **Represent all skin phototypes** (Fitzpatrick I-VI)
4. **Include multiple disease presentations** and stages
5. **Maintain highest ethical standards** throughout
6. **Create sustainable data collection** processes

### Target Conditions
The project focuses on:
- **Leprosy** (Hansen's disease) - all classifications
- **Skin manifestations** of other NTDs as identified
- **Reaction states** and complications
- **Treatment responses** (before/after where appropriate)

### Timeline and Milestones
- **Phase 1**: Pilot data collection (current)
- **Phase 2**: Expanded collection across partners
- **Phase 3**: Dataset completion and AI training
- **Phase 4**: Model validation and deployment
- **Phase 5**: Integration into clinical practice

## Section 5: Your Role and Responsibilities

### Core Responsibilities
As a contributing clinician, you are responsible for:

**1. Patient Care**
- Prioritize patient welfare above all else
- Provide appropriate clinical care regardless of participation
- Respect patient autonomy and decisions
- Maintain professional standards at all times

**2. Informed Consent**
- Explain the project clearly to patients
- Ensure voluntary participation
- Document consent properly
- Allow withdrawal at any time

**3. Image Quality**
- Follow photography guidelines
- Capture clinically useful images
- Complete all required metadata
- Submit only appropriate images

**4. Anonymization**
- Remove all identifying information
- Check images before submission
- Protect patient privacy
- Follow data security protocols

**5. Safeguarding**
- Protect vulnerable patients
- Maintain dignity during photography
- Recognize and report concerns
- Follow institutional protocols

### What You Should NOT Do
- **Never** compromise patient dignity for image quality
- **Never** pressure patients to participate
- **Never** submit images without proper consent
- **Never** share images outside the secure system
- **Never** photograph against patient's wishes
- **Never** include identifying information

### Support Available
You have access to:
- Training materials (this course)
- Technical support from partner organization
- Clinical guidance from specialists
- Ethical consultation when needed
- Institutional resources and protocols

## Section 7: Knowledge Check

**Question 1:** What is the PRIMARY goal of the WHO SkinNTD AI Image Collection Initiative?

A) To train AI systems for improved leprosy and skin NTD diagnosis
B) To replace dermatologists with AI technology
C) To create a commercial database of skin images
D) To develop new medications for leprosy

**Correct Answer:** A
**Explanation:** The initiative aims to build AI diagnostic tools to help healthcare workers in resource-limited settings, not to replace doctors or create commercial products.

**Question 2:** In the governance structure, at which level do you (the clinician) operate?

A) Level 1: WHO oversight
B) Level 2: Partnership management
C) Level 3: Partner Organization - Clinician
D) Level 4: Direct patient relationship

**Correct Answer:** D
**Explanation:** While you're employed/associated with Level 3 (Partner Organization), your direct responsibility is Level 4 - the Clinician-Patient relationship where you have ethical obligations to patient welfare.

**Question 3:** Which of the following is NOT a responsibility of contributing clinicians?

A) Prioritize patient welfare
B) Ensure proper informed consent
C) Share images on social media for awareness
D) Maintain patient dignity

**Correct Answer:** C
**Explanation:** Images must NEVER be shared outside the secure system. This would violate patient privacy and the terms of participation.

**Question 4:** What makes this project ethically sound?

A) It prioritizes patient welfare above data collection
B) It requires informed consent
C) It includes diverse populations
D) All of the above

**Correct Answer:** D
**Explanation:** The project is ethically sound because it maintains patient welfare as primary, requires informed consent, seeks diversity, and follows strict privacy protections.

**Question 5:** True or False: As a contributing clinician, you can withdraw from the project if you have ethical concerns.

**Correct Answer:** True
**Explanation:** You maintain professional autonomy. If you have concerns about ethical aspects of the project, you can discuss them with your supervisor or withdraw while maintaining your duty to existing patients.
