# Module 3: Global Research Consent
## Comprehensive Content Document for Clinical Specialists

### Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, participants will be able to:
1. Distinguish between clinical treatment consent and research data consent.
2. Navigate language and authority barriers without using family members as interpreters.
3. Understand the 4-level governance structure of the AI initiative.
4. Execute dynamic consent by monitoring non-verbal cues.

---

## Section 1: The Transition to Researcher

As a specialist, your relationship with your patient is built on clinical trust. When capturing images for this initiative, you must clearly transition from treating physician to global health researcher. Patients must understand that their data is being used for international algorithm training.

### Governance Structure
The responsibility for this project flows across 4 levels:
- **Level 1 (WHO):** Sets global standards and licenses data for AI.
- **Level 2 (Partner Org):** Manages local operations and data security.
- **Level 3 (Specialist):** You represent your organization and the patient. You hold the direct ethical relationship.
- **Level 4 (Patient):** The ultimate data owner. Their welfare supersedes all data collection milestones.

---

## Section 2: Navigating Consenting Barriers

In countries with high leprosy prevalence, language barriers are common.
- **Valid Consent:** If the patient does not understand your native language, use a professional clinic translator.
- **Avoid Family Translators:** Using family members, especially minors, for global research consent violates ethical standards. The presence of family can introduce power dynamics that prevent a patient from genuinely saying "no."

---

## Section 3: Dynamic Consent

Consent is a conversation, not a signature on a form.
- **Non-Verbal Cues:** If a patient tenses up, stops making eye contact, or pulls their clothing over the lesion while you are photographing, **stop immediately.** Reaffirm their comfort and clarify that their clinical care will continue regardless of participation.

---

## Section 4: Knowledge Check

**Question:** While dealing with a patient presenting severe leprosy nodules, the patient's 14-year-old grandson offers to quickly translate the English AI Consent form into their local dialect. What is your ethical obligation?

A) Allow the grandson to translate, as building the leprosy dataset rapidly is a high global health priority.
B) Decline the offer gently and wait for an official clinic translator, as utilizing minors for global health research consent violates ethical standards.
C) Skip the formal consent since the images will be anonymized anyway.

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Valid consent requires pressure-free comprehension facilitated by an objective translator.

---

## Section 5: Module Summary

### Key Takeaways
1. **Consent Type:** Capture images for global research, not just local clinical records.
2. **Governance:** You are the primary ethical steward for the patient's data.
3. **Barriers:** Always use objective translators, never minors or family members.
4. **Cessation:** Stop if you detect non-verbal withdrawal of consent.
